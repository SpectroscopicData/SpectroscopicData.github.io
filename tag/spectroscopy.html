[2] Benchmark dataset

In this post, we will introduce a spectroscopic benchmark dataset designed for classification. The benchmark consist of large number of LIBS spectra and served for the first classification contest inside LIBS community. We will talk about the contest soon, but now let's focus to the dataset.

Why LIBS?¶

In the case, you are not familiar with Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy (LIBS), it is a method where pulsed laser source is used to ablate the sample and create radiating plasma. This radiation is collected and imaged as a spectrum. The biggest advantage of the method is a speed of analysis (reported up to 1 kHz) and no need for sample preparation. These are the properties, making it ideal for collection of massive amounts of data.

[1] Hello, World! (or what is Spectroscopic Data?)

Welcome to our new blog!¶

In this modern era of data science and machine learning, there are plenty of excellent resources for image processing, natural language processing and other exciting stuff. However, we have found out complete lack of content related to spectroscopic data. So, we have decided to create a platform to gather useful information about the processing of such data and notice you about new ideas in spectroscopy. Also, be prepared for some educational content (python/R code) and occasionally an interview with leading spectroscopists.
